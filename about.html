About

Taniti is a small Pacific island with fewer than 500 square miles of varied terrain, those of which include sandy and rocky beaches, tropical rainforests, a mountainous interior, and an active volcano.

With a population of about 20,000, the island's economy has historically centered around fishing and agriculture but has recently embraced a growing tourism industry.

Frequently Asked Questions

Taniti uses 120-volt power outlets. The same as in the U.S.

Alcohol cannot be sold or served between midnight and 9:00 a.m.

The drinking age is 18, but enforcement is relaxed.
Most younger residents speak fluent English, especially in cities.
There is one hospital and several clinics with multilingual staff.
Violent crime is rare, but petty crime like pickpocketing has increased slightly with tourism.
Many attractions and restaurants close on national holidays. Make sure to plan ahead.
Taniti uses U.S. dollars. Many businesses also accept euros and yen, along with major credit cards.
